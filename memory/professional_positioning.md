## Workshop concept (the independent-practice lane, fleshed out 2026-05-31)

Maurice asked what an initial AI workshop would actually look like. He worries about being out of his depth on deep-technical stuff (not a real developer, shallow on deep n8n, photo/video maybe less relevant) and hates anything sales-y/slimy. The reframe that resolves both worries:

**His perceived weakness IS the product.** The method he described as an aside is the entire curriculum: dictate to Claude Code, be honest about what you don't know, ask for small steps, "teach me like I'm 5" (he ran this all the way through The Fan Orbit's ~40 screens). Non-technical people lack this *posture/workflow*, not the tools. Real developers can't teach it (never experienced being non-technical, don't know where you get stuck); AI "coaches" can't (no operating credibility). He's the rare middle: a credentialed editor who learned to build as a non-engineer and remembers every painful spot. "I'm not a developer, I'm an editor who learned to build real things anyway" is the most disarming, least slimy pitch in that market. Lean INTO the limit; teach only what he genuinely owns and explicitly punt the deep-technical stuff ("not my lane, here's who's good at it" raises credibility).

**Format:** Not a course/funnel/"masterclass." One small live session, a handful of people, 75–90 min, hands-on. Single promise: *you leave with one real thing built, or a repeatable method, not a pile of jargon.* Outcome-anchored is the differentiator — everyone else sells understanding; he sells a thing that exists at the end.

**What he teaches (scoped to his real edge):**
- The posture first and most: how to talk to Claude Code, turn "I don't know" into progress, small steps, "explain like I'm 5," recognizing drift (his editor's eye). The part nobody else can deliver.
- A tiny opinionated stack he actually uses: Claude Code + a WAT-style CLAUDE.md (so the AI behaves like a real collaborator), Perplexity for sourcing (his journalism background makes this uniquely credible), Claude for making something look good.
- Judgment: what's worth building, where AI helps vs. where taste must step in, keeping a project from drifting. His decade of editorial work repackaged — highest-value hour.
- Cut: deep n8n, photo/video, anything he'd bluff on.

**The takeaway artifact:** A better-looking site via Claude is a strong tangible deliverable (tangible = not a webinar). Frame as "I teach the method, the site is proof it worked," NOT "I'll build you a site." On his doubt that "they could've done it without me": technically maybe, but they didn't and won't (they hit the jargon wall and quit). What he sells is **compression** — saving them weeks of flailing and the fear that stops them starting. Honest to say so plainly.

**How to start without being slimy (anti-slime rule: don't broadcast, don't sell transformation, teach in public, let demand pull):**
1. Run ONE free pilot with people who already raised hands (Adena, the senior-social caller). 2–4 people, no charge, "be my guinea pigs." Treat as research, not revenue; learn where they actually get stuck.
2. Let LinkedIn posts be the top of funnel — the translator-style writing demonstrates the thinking, interested people self-select. Opposite of peppering the timeline.
3. Keep the first ask private/small: "running a small first session for a few folks, reply if curious" > any launch announcement.
4. Only AFTER the pilot decide format and price. Don't build the product before live humans show its real shape.

Throughline: not selling a course, sharing a method he actually used, honestly, including what he doesn't know. The honesty is the brand and is exactly what's missing from everyone else.
