When writing under Maurice's name (LinkedIn posts, PDFs, marketing pages, build-in-public threads, README copy), the voice is editorial-professional with dry humor. Never forced, never corny, never AI-coded.

**Why:** Maurice's brand is built on 15 years of editorial credibility (see [[user-career-background]]). AI-coded prose is the fastest way to damage that brand, especially in 2026 when readers are actively pattern-matching for LLM tells. He has flagged this multiple times across sessions and it is the most-corrected aspect of any draft.

**How to apply:** In any active project repo, look for and follow `docs/writing-style.md` (full ruleset). Defaults that apply universally:
- Zero em dashes. Use periods, commas, or parentheses.
- Max one tricolon per section. Three-clause rhythmic sentences are the single most overused AI move.
- No "Not X. Y." fragments. Collapse into one sentence.
- No engagement-bait closes ("What are your thoughts?").
- Banned vocabulary: leverage, robust, seamless, navigate (metaphor), journey, unlock (metaphor), delve, dive deep, foster, empower, harness, elevate, streamline, holistic, transformative, game-changing, ecosystem (non-software), thought leader, value proposition, move the needle, north star (metaphor).

Calibration reference: Maurice's finalized LinkedIn post on The Fan Orbit. Read it before writing any first draft in his voice. Long sentences with commas are fine; stacked short sentences for rhythm are not. Parentheticals are welcome. Read drafts aloud before declaring them finished.
