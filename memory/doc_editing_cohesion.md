When editing any spec, doc, or written artifact for this project, integrate every change as if the document had been written cohesively from scratch. Never append updates as a tacked-on section at the end, and never leave a new section sitting awkwardly next to overlapping existing content. The document must never read as if updates were bolted on.

**Why:** Maurice holds his project documents to an editorial, human-authored quality bar (he's a 15-year senior editor; see [[user-career-background]], [[design-quality-bar]], [[writing-style-rules]]). A bolted-on update is an AI tell and degrades the document. Cohesion is non-negotiable, the same way no-AI-tells is non-negotiable in design and prose. He surfaced this directly after I appended a new pillar section instead of weaving it in, and even flagged my own redundancy rather than resolving it.

**How to apply:** After any edit: (1) weave new content into the correct existing section rather than appending; (2) merge or eliminate redundant/adjacent-overlapping sections the change creates; (3) update intro framing, phasing, cross-references, and anything downstream so the whole reads as one coherent piece; (4) re-read the full document end to end to confirm it flows as a single authored work, not v1-plus-patches. Relocate and restructure freely in service of cohesion; this overrides the usual "surgical, touch-only-what-you-must" default for documents (it still applies to code). Ties to [[honest-dialogue]]: fix problems I spot, don't just narrate them.
