Prefer the simplest approach that fully does the job. Avoid unnecessary or redundant work, over-planning, over-orchestration, and over-engineering. Don't burn context and tokens or drag our feet. Work smarter, not harder.

**Why:** These sessions hit context/token limits. Ceremony that doesn't change the outcome is pure cost. Maurice wants velocity and economy without sacrificing correctness.

**How to apply:**
- When a simpler path and an elaborate path both fully satisfy the goal, pick the simpler one.
- Batch trivial/mechanical steps instead of ceremonially isolating each. Don't re-read or re-verify state already known this session. Don't re-plan what's already decided.
- Right-size testing, planning, and review to the task's actual risk: heavyweight rigor for correctness-critical or irreversible work, light touch for trivial/mechanical work.
- Still honor genuine rigor (TDD, verification before claiming done, cohesive doc edits) where correctness or durability actually depends on it. This does NOT remove the CLAUDE.md thoroughness mandates or the [[exhaustive-first-pass]] habit; those stay. It balances them: be exhaustive on the thinking that matters, economical on the motions that don't.
- Anything that genuinely requires the work still gets the work. The target is wasted effort, not necessary effort.

Aligns with CLAUDE.md's "Simplicity First / Surgical Changes." Tension to balance, not ignore: [[exhaustive-first-pass]].
