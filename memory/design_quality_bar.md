Maurice has made beauty a non-negotiable requirement for any product, marketing surface, or visual work shipped under his name. The bar is Apple-level: restraint, confidence, type and space doing the design work, every element justifying its existence.

**Why:** Generic visual work undercuts the editorial brand he's spent fifteen years building. He also wants any surface he ships to differentiate against the dozens of AI-template-feeling products launching in the same window. He is explicitly aware of the AI design tropes that have become defaults (purple palettes, gradient everything, glassmorphism, Corporate Memphis illustrations) and considers them disqualifying.

**How to apply:** Read `docs/design-principles.md` before any visual decision. The doc enumerates the banned tropes and the affirmative bar. Quick reference of the headline rules: no purple anywhere in the brand palette, no gradient backgrounds or borders, no glassmorphism, no sidebar accent bars on cards, no stock photography or faceless illustrated humans, no animated wave dividers, no auto-playing video, no "trusted by" logo greys. Two type families maximum. Custom easing curves, not default linear. The test that catches generic work: "could this be on a SaaS landing page for any other product?" If yes, rebuild. See also [[writing-style-rules]] for the language equivalent.
