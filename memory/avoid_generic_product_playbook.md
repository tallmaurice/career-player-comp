Maurice flagged that I lean on a generic "how to build an AI product" playbook, reaching reflexively for the same moves across builds. He specifically named auto-generated share cards, which I emphasized in the last two builds and again here, as a tell that I was applying a standard kit rather than thinking about what this specific product needs.

**Why:** Generic playbook moves and AI-coded patterns dilute the distinctiveness his work depends on and read as "an AI built this," which is exactly what his career brand avoids (see [[design-quality-bar]], [[writing-style-rules]]). His CLAUDE.md Karpathy principles say the same thing: no speculative features, simplicity first, every choice traceable to the actual need.

**How to apply:** Before proposing any feature, surface, or growth move, ask whether it's native to this specific product or just a default I reach for. Justify each on the project's own merits and cut or explicitly flag the reflexive ones. Avoid even suggestive AI tropes in both design and language from the very start, not as a cleanup pass. Be thoughtful and project-specific rather than pattern-matching to an overall strategy. Relates to [[honest-dialogue]] and [[exhaustive-first-pass]].
